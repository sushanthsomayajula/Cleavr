qFlow: receptor quantification & subtype validation

Enter calibration bead runs and flow cytometry measurements below. This tool fits a per-date calibration curve (linear or log-log, whichever fits better), converts raw MFI into absolute antibody binding capacity (receptors per cell), and compares the protein-level pattern across TNBC subtypes against the RNA-level pattern from the computational screen. Same math as receptor_quantification.py and flow_cytometry_validation.py, done here so nothing has to round-trip through a spreadsheet.

Calibration and ABC logic mirrors code/receptor_quantification.py exactly (manual OLS, linear vs. log-log selection by R²). Subtype logic mirrors code/flow_cytometry_validation.py, but only ever uses calibrated receptors-per-cell. A measurement row without a fitted calibration curve for its date is excluded from the subtype comparison and flagged in the log, never approximated from the raw MFI/isotype ratio, since that ratio has no absolute meaning on its own. Nothing on this page is sent anywhere: all computation happens locally in your browser. CSV import/export uses the same column schema as the Python scripts, so files are interchangeable. See Methodology below for the exact equations and references.

Methodology: equations & references

1. Calibration curve fit

For each calibration date, ordinary least squares is fit twice, once on raw values (linear model) and once on log10-transformed values (log-log model), and whichever fit has the higher R² is kept and reported. Implemented as explicit summations, not a stats library, so every step is auditable.

Linear model: ABC = a·MFI + b Log-log model: log10(ABC) = a·log10(MFI) + b => ABC = 10^(a·log10(MFI) + b) Ordinary least squares (both models fit this way, x/y substituted accordingly): mean_x = (1/n) * sum(x_i) mean_y = (1/n) * sum(y_i) a (slope) = sum((x_i - mean_x)(y_i - mean_y)) / sum((x_i - mean_x)^2) b (intercept) = mean_y - a * mean_x R^2 = 1 - [ sum((y_i - y_pred_i)^2) / sum((y_i - mean_y)^2) ] Minimum 3 bead populations per date (kits typically ship 4-6). R^2 below 0.95 is flagged as noisier than kit manufacturers typically expect.

2. Antibody binding capacity (ABC) conversion

ABC_target = curve applied to target-antibody MFI ABC_isotype = curve applied to isotype-control MFI Net receptors per cell = ABC_target - ABC_isotype This background-subtraction step is what makes the number a receptor count and not just a fluorescence readout: isotype MFI is the assay's own noise floor (nonspecific antibody binding), and subtracting it out is standard practice for antibody-binding-capacity flow cytometry (qFlow).

3. Subtype comparison

Mean calibrated receptors/cell is averaged per cell line, then per subtype (BL1/BL2/M/LAR, Lehmann classification). Subtypes are ranked low-to-high on the protein side and compared against the same ranking on the RNA side (TCGA medians from biomarker_pipeline.py). Cell lines not in the lookup table, and measurement rows without a calibration curve for their date, are excluded and flagged in the log rather than silently dropped or approximated.
