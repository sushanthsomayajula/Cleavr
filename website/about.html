Cleavr · About

One person, testing one claim at a time — in the open.

Cleavr isn't a lab, a company, or a funded team yet. It's an independent project, and this page exists so that's never hidden.

01Why this exists

One question that didn't have a systematic answer.

TNBC subtype-specific biomarker testing is usually done ad hoc — one gene, one paper, one lab, as it comes up. Cleavr started from a single, narrow question: does GNRHR, the target of an active TNBC nanoparticle-targeting strategy, actually express differently across TNBC's molecular subtypes? Answering that properly — independently re-deriving the numbers, checking them against the literature, reporting the null result honestly — turned into the case study this site opened with. Building the tooling to ask the same question about any gene, not just that one, is what turned it into Cleavr.

02Who's building it

Independent, early-stage, and upfront about both.

Cleavr is built by Sushanth Somayajula, a college sophomore, as an independent project — not affiliated with a university lab, company, or research group. That's precisely why every claim on this site is independently re-derived from public data (TCGA, ChEMBL, PubMed, Open Targets) and cross-checked against the literature before it's published: the site's credibility has to come from the methodology being auditable, not from institutional reputation it doesn't have yet.

03How it's built

Same rules as the rest of the site.

Every number is independently re-derived from raw source files in a second pass, not computed once and trusted. Every citation is checked against the primary source before it's used. Null results are reported as directly as positive ones. Code, data processing steps, and full methodology are public on GitHub — nothing here is meant to be taken on reputation alone.
